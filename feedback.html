Feedback & Support Directory

Building peace and justice requires more than awareness. People also need clear places to go for support, dialogue, and guidance. This page highlights practical community programmes linked to SDG 16 and allows visitors to share their experience, suggestions, and feedback through a structured form.

Programme Directory

The following programmes show how schools, communities, and support services can make justice more visible, accessible, and inclusive. Scroll through the directory to explore different types of support.

Community Legal Aid Clinic

A support programme that connects individuals with basic legal guidance, referral pathways, and rights awareness sessions. It is especially useful for people who may feel overwhelmed by legal language or formal procedures.

Youth Civic Dialogue Hub

A student-focused initiative that encourages respectful debate, leadership, public discussion, and stronger understanding of democratic participation. It helps young people practise peaceful communication in shared spaces.

Digital Justice Access Desk

A digital-first support channel that helps users find trustworthy public resources, reporting pathways, and guidance in a more accessible and less intimidating format. It is valuable for people who prefer remote support.

Neighbourhood Mediation Circle

A local programme designed to reduce everyday conflict through guided conversation, listening, and problem-solving. This kind of initiative can improve trust and reduce the need for situations to escalate.
